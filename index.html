The Position

The Vice President's Responsibility

"The Vice President shall supervise the AECE Intern Program, represent the organization as needed, and assume the duties of the President in the President's absence to ensure continuity of leadership."

Our current Vice President Joseph described the role plainly: the VP is the operational backbone of the organization, ensuring that commitments are honored, programs run effectively, and the e-board moves with purpose. Through my work with Aggie Academy, the Girl Scouts, and consistent adherence to COP submission protocols, I have been developing exactly that kind of disciplined, mission-oriented work ethic.

Qualifications

Three Semesters as a Teaching Assistant

Serving as an undergraduate TA has required more than academic proficiency; it has required genuine investment in the development of others. I have spent three semesters meeting students where they are, breaking down complex material, and showing up consistently for people who needed guidance.

That same commitment to mentorship is exactly what the intern program deserves from its VP. I understand that intern development is not transactional, it is relational. I intend to lead it accordingly.

My Initiatives

Two Initiatives, If Elected.

Initiative 01

Deeper Integration of Interns into E-Board Operations

Beyond the existing weekly officer check-ins, I want to create a structured layer in which interns attend e-board meetings and have the opportunity to contribute to tasks aligned with their interests. This gives interns direct exposure to the roles they may one day seek — and equips them with real context heading into campaign season should they choose to run.

Initiative 02

Intentional One-on-One Relationship Building

AECE's mission is grounded in community, and community is built through genuine connection. I intend to prioritize individual relationships with members — not just visibility, but real engagement — and to actively encourage members to step into volunteer and core roles. If people feel seen and valued, they show up.

Personal Reflection

What AECE Has Meant to Me.

AECE has been one of the most formative parts of my time at NCA&T. This organization gave me an environment to grow, a mission worth showing up for, and relationships that genuinely feel like family. I am not running for Vice President to hold a title. I am running because this organization has given me something real, and I want to give something meaningful back to it.
